88 WEST 108TH STREET

PROJECT TYPE: RESIDENTIAL | LOCATION: NEW YORK, NY | SIZE: 12,000 SF| STATUS: IN PROGRESS

Nestled in the Upper West Side of Manhattan, a mere block from the American Museum of Natural History and just two blocks from the sprawling beauty of Central Park, stands a distinguished 1890 landmarked brownstone. This architectural gem has undergone a meticulous retrofitting, seamlessly blending the charm of its Renaissance Revival façade with the modern sophistication of a single-family residence.

While the exterior exudes timeless elegance, the interior has undergone a comprehensive transformation. The first-floor entrance unfolds into a grand, double-height parlor, setting the tone for the residence's expansive and welcoming atmosphere. A hallway discreetly leads to the more private library space at the rear of the house, providing a serene retreat.

Ascending through the second to fourth floors reveals thoughtfully designed bedrooms, each equipped with its full bathroom. On each floor, well-appointed kitchens and shared common spaces encourage a sense of community and interaction. Above the existing brownstone, a two-story penthouse addition crowns the structure, housing the luxurious master suite.

At the garden level, a service entrance opens to a full kitchen, while a connecting hallway leads to the dining room, seamlessly transitioning to the backyard for al fresco dining. The house's entertainment and fitness offerings anchor the subterranean levels, boasting a wine cellar, multimedia room, gym, racquetball court, saunas, and a pool—a haven for recreation and relaxation.

A marvel of modern design, the house is connected by a cantilevered staircase enclosed in continuous glass walls, creating a striking visual element. Additionally, a commercial elevator at the building's core ensures seamless vertical movement, enhancing accessibility throughout this impeccably retrofitted brownstone—a harmonious blend of historic charm and contemporary luxury.
